Short answers to the questions that come up most before and during setup.

> **Beta note:** Answers below reflect the current beta and may be refined
> before launch.

## What is MCP?

The Model Context Protocol is an open standard that lets AI clients call
tools on a remote server. Ranklens is an MCP server — your client calls its
tools directly, inside your own chat.

## Which clients work?

Claude Desktop, claude.ai, Claude Code, Cursor, and Windsurf. See
[Getting started](/docs/getting-started) for setup steps per client.

## Do I need Google Search Console?

No. Your first crawl and audit run without it. Connecting GSC unlocks
performance-based tools like decay analysis — see
[GSC connection](/docs/core-concepts/gsc-connection).

## What do credits cost?

Costs are draft numbers while we calibrate against real usage — see
[/pricing](/pricing) for the current table.

## Is my data used to train AI models?

No. Your site data is never used to train AI models — see
[Security](/docs/security).

## When does beta open?

We're inviting people from the waitlist in small batches. Join the waitlist
on the homepage to get in line.

## How do I delete my data?

Reach us via the waitlist email you used and ask for a deletion. It's a
full purge — see [Data retention](/docs/core-concepts/data-retention).
